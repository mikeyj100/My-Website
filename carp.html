Carp Task

This task requires research of the following design terms: contrast, repetition, proximity and alignment. In addition to this, application of the design terms had to be presented through a film festival poster

Using design terms such as contrast, alignment, proximity and repetition in a media form such as posters can be essential to attract the human eye. Contrast can determine the importance of whatever information is displayed. Regarding a film festival, information that will need to stand out would be the festival name, date and location. Alignment is essential too because when writing is central it is more natural on the eye. It can be argued proximity and repetition are the least important of the four as it is present in less media forms and is hard too perfect and seen as a more artistic and unique style of design which varies in effectiveness.

This poster shows strong elements of contrast as the white is strikingly different from the rest highlighting the importance of the festival name
The proximity included in my second poster stands out as 3 angled squares are seen near eachother creating a cube
In my final poster allignment is present as the four squares (two invisible) are in correct relative positions
